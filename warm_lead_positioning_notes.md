# Warm Lead Positioning Notes

These notes capture the current intent for the landing page. The page is not meant to cold-sell strangers. It is meant to support an in-person conversation when someone asks to see a website.

## Primary Visitor

The most likely visitor is a warm lead who met Marko in person and asked for a website afterward.

They are usually a non-technical person with ideas for apps or software they want to build, but they do not know how to turn those ideas into something real.

Examples:

- Working professionals
- College students
- Recruiters
- Marketers
- Nurses
- Other domain experts with app ideas

They are not necessarily startup CEOs or engineering leaders. They are more likely people with domain knowledge, curiosity, and ideas, but without the technical confidence to build.

## Current Situation

They have ideas for apps, automations, or software products, but they feel blocked.

Common beliefs and fears:

- Their idea feels too big.
- They feel like they need an engineer.
- They feel overwhelmed by the tools.
- They do not know where to start.
- They do not know how to break the idea down into something buildable.
- They may have a business idea, but they do not know how to turn it into software.

## Program Container

The program is a two-month / 8-week program.

Earlier docs may say 90 days, but the current direction is 8 weeks. The program changed when the "30 apps in 30 days" concept was replaced with building one app per week.

The current program should be described as an 8-week build cycle, not a one-day workshop, a three-session sprint, or a 90-day program.

There are no cohorts. Avoid "next cohort" language.

## Weekly Build Rhythm

The program is focused on weekly building across 8 weeks.

The participant still has one core app they are working toward. The weekly rhythm should not create the impression that the program is mainly about building a different app every week.

Each week includes one 24-hour sprint. The point is to drill the building muscle, practice the workflow, and make building feel repeatable while still keeping the participant's core app as the main focus.

This should probably be explained more clearly on a call than emphasized heavily before the call.

## Price

The current price is $7,500.

However, the page may not need to show the price. Since this is a high-ticket program and the page is for warm leads, showing the price too early may create unnecessary friction before the value is fully understood.

The page should likely move away from direct checkout language and toward a call-based next step.

## CTA Direction

The goal of the page is to schedule a call.

Possible next steps:

- Book a call
- Fill out an application form
- Pick a date for a conversation

Direct checkout may not be ideal for a $7,500 warm-lead offer. The page should probably support the sales conversation rather than try to close the sale by itself.

Use "Schedule a call" as the primary CTA.

## Outcome

The outcome is not "your app with paid users."

Paid users are not part of the guarantee and should not be implied.

The real outcome is closer to:

- Build and launch real apps with AI.
- Put software in front of real users.
- Learn the repeatable skill of building apps independently.
- Become someone who can turn ideas into working software without waiting on an engineer.
- Walk away with cutting-edge AI app-building workflows.
- Learn prompt engineering, app design, and enough software development lifecycle thinking to build more confidently.

The page should emphasize skill acquisition and repeated building, not a revenue guarantee.

## Promise Boundaries

The page should not promise:

- Paid users
- Revenue
- A guaranteed business outcome
- That the program replaces all engineering needs forever
- That every participant will launch a polished company-ready product

The page can promise:

- A structured 8-week build process
- A repeatable AI app-building workflow
- Support getting unstuck
- Building and shipping weekly
- Increased technical confidence
- Real software built around the participant's own ideas
- Lifetime access to the program content
- Weekly calls
- Support between calls so participants do not stay stuck for days
- Work on the participant's own idea, not generic course projects

## Who This Is Not For

This is not for CEOs buying training for their engineering teams.

This is not primarily for businesses trying to automate internal sales, operations, or admin workflows.

This is not an enterprise AI enablement program.

This is not a general business automation consulting offer.

## Who This Is For

This is for non-engineers who want to build apps, automations, or software that could become businesses.

They may be working professionals, students, or domain experts. The common thread is that they have ideas and want to become capable of building them without being dependent on an engineer from day one.

## Page Strategy

The page should make the program feel credible, structured, and serious after someone has already met Marko.

It should not try to do heavy cold-sales persuasion.

The page should answer:

- Is this real?
- Is this structured?
- Is this for someone like me?
- Will this help me stop feeling blocked?
- What is the next step if I want to explore it?

The page should avoid:

- Workshop language
- Direct checkout framing
- Overpromising business outcomes
- Positioning the program as corporate training
- Over-indexing on teams, CTO replacement, or enterprise ROI
